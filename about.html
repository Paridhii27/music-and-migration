Music is a powerful tool for preserving and transmitting cultural memory, especially for displaced communities. In the early years there was little interest in the phenomenon of migration in regards to music other than how it related to the diffusion of artefacts such as musical instruments from one culture to another. However, over the years migrants have now been identified as catalysts for cultural exchange leading to fusion of music styles and emergence of new musical genres. Songs are repositories of shared experiences, values, and historical narratives. It possesses a unique ability to evoke memories and emotions that transcend language barriers. What are examples of memories which are preserved in music that emerges from migrations of people? What kind of emotions do these memories evoke?
