“Ethnomusicology is the study of why, and how human beings are musical… “Musical” in this definition does not refer to musical talent or ability, rather it refers to the capacity of humans to create, perform, organize cognitively, react physically and emotionally to, and interpret the meaning of humanly organized sounds.” (Rice, 2014, p. 1) Sounds and music are inherent to human nature. Ethnomusicology looks at music in relation to its cultural anthropology, geographical and historical extent, social contexts as well as biological factors. Through the perspective of ethnomusicology music is inherent to human nature. So how does this music transmit human experiences and feelings across spatial and temporal distances? Can music be a unifying factor bringing together communities from disparate lands? How does music shift and evolve as it crosses spatial boundaries through the migration of communities of people and what are the kinds of memories embedded in such music?
